About

Real food. Real households. No spreadsheets.

We build meal plans for the way people actually live — with kids who have football practice, partners with their own goals, and Tuesdays where dinner needs to be on the table in fifteen minutes. One household. One plan. Everyone fed.

Our mission

Most meal planners treat people like spreadsheets with a stomach. We don't. We built Meal Plan Pro because the gap between "what should I eat this week?" and actually getting it on the table is wider than any nutrition app has bothered to bridge.

Especially when you're feeding a family, training for something, or just trying to stop ordering takeaway on a Wednesday.

What we believe

Six things we refuse to compromise on.

These aren't aspirations. They're the rules we hold ourselves to when we ship anything.

1

Plans should match real life.

Tuesday breakfast isn't shakshuka. Friday's a takeaway. Saturday is for proper cooking. We bake the rhythm of a real week into every plan — quick weekday breakfasts, leisurely weekends, lighter days when you're eating out.

2

Households eat together.

Two adults with different goals. Children who can't have what's spicy. School lunches we don't shop for. We plan for the whole table, not just one person at it — with portion scaling, shared meals, and quantities that fill the trolley.

3

Training deserves real fuel.

Carb load on Saturday. Recovery within forty-five minutes. Race-week protocol that respects your gut. If you're training for something, the plan periodises with you — not against you.

4

You stay in charge.

Don't fancy the salmon? Swap it. Hate Wednesday's lunch? Replace it. Nothing locks in until you approve the week. Then — and only then — the shopping list is built around the plan you actually chose.

5

Cost is part of the plan.

Three tiers, real prices, honest totals. Per person, per day, per month. Aisle-ordered shopping. Tappable links straight to your trolley. Because a plan you can't afford is a plan you won't follow.

6

Privacy is non-negotiable.

Your household data lives in EU-region encrypted storage. Your account is yours. We don't sell data, don't run third-party trackers, and don't pretend otherwise.

Who we serve

Built for the household kitchen.

If your week looks like one of these, we've designed for you.

A

The busy household

Two adults, kids in tow, fifteen minutes on a Wednesday. Plans that respect the calendar — quick mornings, school lunches handled, a takeaway built into the budget without blowing the week.

B

The training partnership

One of you is marathon training. The other isn't. Both of you eat from the same kitchen. We periodise meals independently — your fuel, their balance, one shopping list.

C

The solo cook

Living alone shouldn't mean living on toast. Right-sized portions, no waste, plans that scale up when guests come over and back down when they leave.

A note for practitioners

We're built for households, not clinics.

If you're a registered nutritionist or dietitian managing a caseload of clients, you'll want a platform with audit trails, white-labelling, and clinical oversight — and there are excellent ones already out there. We respect that work, and we don't try to replicate it. Meal Plan Pro is the tool you'd recommend a client use between sessions, or for the partner you're not formally treating.
